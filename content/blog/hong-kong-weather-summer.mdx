## Overview

Hong Kong, China — summer is hot, humid, and prone to typhoons, but the city's air-conditioned malls, incredible food, and dramatic storms have their own appeal. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **32°C (90°F)** while nights cool to **27°C (81°F)**. Expect around 6 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Hong Kong sees about **380mm of rainfall across 16 days** during this period. Downpours can be heavy but usually pass quickly. Waterproof gear is essential.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **82%**. Combined with the heat this can feel oppressive. Stay hydrated, wear breathable fabrics, and take advantage of air-conditioned spaces during peak afternoon hours.

## What to Pack

- Lightweight breathable clothing in cotton or linen
- High-SPF sunscreen, sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat
- Waterproof jacket and a sturdy umbrella
- Moisture-wicking fabrics and a refillable water bottle
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Take the Peak Tram to Victoria Peak for skyline views
- Sample dim sum at a traditional Cantonese teahouse
- Ride the Star Ferry across Victoria Harbour at night
- Visit the Ten Thousand Buddhas Monastery in Sha Tin

## Travel Tips

- Check the Hong Kong Observatory for typhoon warnings before heading out
- Carry an umbrella everywhere — afternoon downpours strike with little warning
- Bring a light layer for aggressively air-conditioned MTR trains and malls
