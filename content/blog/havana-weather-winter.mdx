## Overview

Havana, Cuba — winter is Havana's dry season — warm, breezy, and ideal for wandering the pastel-coloured streets of Old Havana. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **27°C (81°F)** while nights cool to **18°C (64°F)**. Expect around 8 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Havana sees about **45mm of rainfall across 5 days** during this period. Rainfall is moderate. The odd shower is possible but unlikely to ruin your plans.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **70%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Light layers — t-shirts plus a jacket for cooler evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Stroll the Malecón seawall at sunset with the locals
- Explore the crumbling beauty of Old Havana, a UNESCO World Heritage site
- Ride in a classic 1950s American convertible along the seafront
- Day-trip to Viñales Valley for tobacco farms and limestone mogotes

## Travel Tips

- Cash is king — bring euros or Canadian dollars to exchange; US cards often do not work
- WiFi is limited; buy ETECSA cards at parks and hotel lobbies
- Jineteros (street touts) are persistent but polite; a firm 'no gracias' works
