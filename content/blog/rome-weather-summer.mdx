## Overview

Rome, Italy — summer brings blazing heat, but also the longest days, outdoor dining, and a chance to explore ancient ruins in dramatic golden light. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **32°C (90°F)** while nights cool to **19°C (66°F)**. Expect around 11 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Rome sees about **15mm of rainfall across 2 days** during this period. Rain is uncommon, though a light sprinkle is not out of the question.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **50%**. The air is pleasantly dry, making even warmer temperatures feel manageable.

## What to Pack

- Lightweight breathable clothing in cotton or linen
- High-SPF sunscreen, sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Visit the Colosseum and Forum at opening time to beat the heat and crowds
- Tour the Vatican Museums and Sistine Chapel on a Wednesday morning
- Cool off with artisan gelato from a gelateria in Trastevere
- Stroll through Villa Borghese gardens in the shade of umbrella pines

## Travel Tips

- Hydration is key — carry a water bottle and refill at Rome's many nasoni fountains
- Many Romans leave in August so some local restaurants close; tourist areas stay open
- Free entry to state museums on the first Sunday of each month
