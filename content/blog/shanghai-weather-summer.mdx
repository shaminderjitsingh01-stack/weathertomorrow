## Overview

Shanghai, China — summer in Shanghai is hot and steamy with the plum rain season in June giving way to intense heat in July and August. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **32°C (90°F)** while nights cool to **25°C (77°F)**. Expect around 7 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Shanghai sees about **160mm of rainfall across 12 days** during this period. Downpours can be heavy but usually pass quickly. Waterproof gear is essential.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **80%**. Combined with the heat this can feel oppressive. Stay hydrated, wear breathable fabrics, and take advantage of air-conditioned spaces during peak afternoon hours.

## What to Pack

- Lightweight breathable clothing in cotton or linen
- High-SPF sunscreen, sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat
- Waterproof jacket and a sturdy umbrella
- Moisture-wicking fabrics and a refillable water bottle
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Walk the Bund at night when the Pudong skyline lights up across the river
- Explore the traditional Yu Garden and its surrounding bazaar
- Stroll the tree-lined streets of the French Concession
- Take in the Pudong skyline from the Shanghai Tower observation deck

## Travel Tips

- The plum rain season (meiyu) in June brings persistent drizzle — waterproofs are essential
- Air conditioning is fierce indoors; carry a light layer for malls and restaurants
- The Shanghai Metro is extensive and easy to navigate with English signage
