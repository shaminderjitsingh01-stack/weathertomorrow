## Overview

Munich, Germany — October is Oktoberfest — the world's largest beer festival fills the city with lederhosen, brass bands, and enormous pretzels. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **13°C (55°F)** while nights cool to **4°C (39°F)**. Expect around 4 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Munich sees about **55mm of rainfall across 10 days** during this period. Rainfall is moderate. The odd shower is possible but unlikely to ruin your plans.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **75%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Layered outfits — sweaters, a warm jacket, and a scarf
- Waterproof walking shoes
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Experience Oktoberfest at the Theresienwiese fairgrounds
- Walk through the English Garden, one of the world's largest urban parks
- Visit Marienplatz to see the Glockenspiel chime at 11 am
- Tour the BMW Museum for Bavarian engineering history

## Travel Tips

- Reserve a beer tent table months ahead; showing up without one means long queues
- Oktoberfest actually starts in September — the last weekend falls in early October
- Dress warmly for evening; temperatures drop fast after sunset in the beer gardens
