## Overview

Santiago, Chile — the dry Mediterranean climate and dramatic Andes backdrop make Santiago a year-round destination with distinct seasons. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **22°C (72°F)** while nights cool to **8°C (46°F)**. Expect around 8 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Santiago sees about **30mm of rainfall across 4 days** during this period. Rain is uncommon, though a light sprinkle is not out of the question.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **55%**. The air is pleasantly dry, making even warmer temperatures feel manageable.

## What to Pack

- Light layers — t-shirts plus a jacket for cooler evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Hike or take the funicular up Cerro San Cristóbal for Andes views
- Explore the Mercado Central for fresh seafood and Chilean empanadas
- Tour the Maipo Valley wine region just outside the city
- Day-trip to Cajón del Maipo for hot springs and canyon scenery

## Travel Tips

- Smog can be heavy in winter (June-August) due to temperature inversions
- Summer (December-February) is best for outdoor activities and wine tours
- The metro is fast and clean — the best way to cross the city
