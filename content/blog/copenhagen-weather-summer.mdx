## Overview

Copenhagen, Denmark — summer brings the best of Danish hygge outdoors — long daylight hours, canal-side dining, and a city perfectly designed for cycling. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **21°C (70°F)** while nights cool to **13°C (55°F)**. Expect around 9 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Copenhagen sees about **60mm of rainfall across 9 days** during this period. Rainfall is moderate. The odd shower is possible but unlikely to ruin your plans.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **65%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Light layers — t-shirts plus a jacket for cooler evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Visit the colourful Nyhavn waterfront for photos and canal-boat tours
- Spend an evening at Tivoli Gardens amusement park
- Cycle through Christiania, the self-governing neighbourhood
- Tour the Danish Design Museum and Royal Copenhagen porcelain

## Travel Tips

- A Copenhagen Card covers transport, 80+ attractions, and canal tours
- Rent a bike — the city has dedicated cycle lanes everywhere
- Even in summer, bring a jacket for cool evenings near the harbour
