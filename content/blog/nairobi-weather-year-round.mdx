## Overview

Nairobi, Kenya — sitting at 1,700m altitude, Nairobi enjoys a surprisingly temperate climate — warm days, cool nights, and Africa's wildlife right on the city's doorstep. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **25°C (77°F)** while nights cool to **13°C (55°F)**. Expect around 7 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Nairobi sees about **90mm of rainfall across 10 days** during this period. Showers are fairly regular — keep an umbrella handy when you head out.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **60%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Light layers — t-shirts plus a jacket for cooler evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Visit Nairobi National Park — the only national park inside a capital city
- Feed giraffes at the Giraffe Centre in Lang'ata
- Explore the Karen Blixen Museum and its colonial history
- Take a day trip to the Great Rift Valley viewpoint

## Travel Tips

- Long rains fall March-May, short rains October-December — June to September is driest
- Evenings get genuinely cool; bring a fleece or light jacket
- Kenyan coffee is world-class — visit a local roastery
