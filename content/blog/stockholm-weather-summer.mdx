## Overview

Stockholm, Sweden — summer is Stockholm's golden season — nearly 19 hours of daylight, warm enough for swimming, and the entire city comes alive outdoors. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **22°C (72°F)** while nights cool to **13°C (55°F)**. Expect around 10 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Stockholm sees about **65mm of rainfall across 9 days** during this period. Rainfall is moderate. The odd shower is possible but unlikely to ruin your plans.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **60%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Light layers — t-shirts plus a jacket for cooler evenings
- Sunscreen and sunglasses
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Explore the medieval alleyways of Gamla Stan (Old Town)
- Take a boat through the Stockholm archipelago's 30,000 islands
- Visit the ABBA Museum and the Vasa Museum on Djurgården
- Swim in the clean waters of Lake Mälaren or the Baltic shoreline

## Travel Tips

- Book accommodation early — Midsummer (late June) is the biggest holiday
- A Stockholm Pass covers free boat tours and many museum admissions
- Pack for variable weather; a warm pullover is needed even in July evenings
