## Overview

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil — summer means Carnival energy, packed beaches, and tropical heat — this is Rio at its most vibrant and exuberant. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **32°C (90°F)** while nights cool to **24°C (75°F)**. Expect around 7 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Rio de Janeiro sees about **170mm of rainfall across 12 days** during this period. Downpours can be heavy but usually pass quickly. Waterproof gear is essential.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **78%**. Combined with the heat this can feel oppressive. Stay hydrated, wear breathable fabrics, and take advantage of air-conditioned spaces during peak afternoon hours.

## What to Pack

- Lightweight breathable clothing in cotton or linen
- High-SPF sunscreen, sunglasses, and a wide-brimmed hat
- Waterproof jacket and a sturdy umbrella
- Moisture-wicking fabrics and a refillable water bottle
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Lay out on Copacabana or Ipanema Beach at golden hour
- Take the cog train up to Cristo Redentor for panoramic views
- Ride the cable car to the top of Sugarloaf Mountain at sunset
- Experience samba at a local bar in Lapa on a Friday night

## Travel Tips

- Afternoon thunderstorms are almost daily — mornings are the best time for outdoor plans
- Keep valuables at your hotel when at the beach
- Carnival dates shift yearly — book 6 months ahead if visiting during the festival
