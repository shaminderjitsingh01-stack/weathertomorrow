## The 20°C Sweet Spot

Twenty degrees Celsius. It's not hot. It's not cold. It's the temperature where you can get it perfectly right — or perfectly wrong.

Wear too much and you're peeling off layers by noon. Wear too little and you're shivering at 8 AM waiting for the bus. This guide breaks down exactly what works at 20°C.

## Morning vs Afternoon

The tricky part about 20°C is that it's usually the **high** for the day. That means:

- **Morning (7–9 AM)**: It's probably 12–15°C. You need a jacket.
- **Midday (12–2 PM)**: It hits 20°C. The jacket comes off.
- **Evening (6–8 PM)**: It drops to 15–16°C. The jacket goes back on.

This is why **layering** is the only correct answer at 20°C.

## The Core Outfit

Here's the formula that works every time:

### Top Half
- **T-shirt or light blouse** — cotton or linen, your choice
- **Light jacket** — denim jacket, thin bomber, unlined blazer, or a cotton hoodie
- That's it. Two layers. Simple.

### Bottom Half
- **Jeans or chinos** — standard weight, not thermal
- Shorts work if the low is above 16°C and you're comfortable
- Avoid heavy denim (too warm by midday)

### Footwear
- **Closed shoes** — sneakers, loafers, low boots
- Sandals are fine if the sun is out and you're not walking long distances
- Avoid heavy boots — your feet will overheat

### Accessories
- **Sunglasses** — the sun at 20°C can be surprisingly strong, especially in spring
- **Light scarf** — optional, useful if there's wind
- **Umbrella** — if rain probability is above 40%, bring one. Check the forecast.

## Humid 20°C vs Dry 20°C

This is where most guides fail. Twenty degrees in **Singapore** feels completely different from 20°C in **Madrid**.

### Humid 20°C (Singapore, Bangkok, Miami)

Humidity above 70% makes 20°C feel warmer and stickier.

- Skip the jacket — you won't need it
- Loose-fitting cotton or moisture-wicking fabrics
- Light colours to reflect heat
- Breathable shoes (mesh sneakers or sandals)
- Feels like: 22–24°C

### Dry 20°C (Madrid, Denver, Johannesburg)

Humidity below 40% makes 20°C feel crisp and pleasant.

- Keep the light jacket for morning and shade
- Any fabric works — cotton, linen, even light wool
- Standard sneakers or leather shoes
- Feels like: 18–20°C

## What to Skip at 20°C

- **Heavy coats** — you'll carry them all day
- **Thermal underwear** — way too much
- **Thick sweaters** — a thin knit is the maximum
- **Flip-flops** (unless you're at the beach)
- **Heavy scarves and beanies** — it's not that cold

## Quick Decision Table

| Condition | What to Wear |
|---|---|
| 20°C, sunny, low humidity | T-shirt + jeans, sunglasses |
| 20°C, cloudy, low humidity | T-shirt + light jacket + jeans |
| 20°C, rainy | T-shirt + waterproof jacket + jeans + umbrella |
| 20°C, sunny, high humidity | Loose cotton t-shirt + shorts or light trousers |
| 20°C, windy | T-shirt + windbreaker + jeans |

## The Bottom Line

At 20°C, the t-shirt and light jacket combo is your best friend. Keep it simple, check if rain is expected, and adjust for humidity.
