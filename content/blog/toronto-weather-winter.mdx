## Overview

Toronto, Canada — winter is genuinely cold with frequent snowfall, but the underground PATH network, world-class museums, and hockey culture keep the city buzzing. Whether you are planning a holiday or a business trip, understanding the weather helps you pack right and make the most of your time.

## Temperature

Daytime highs reach **-1°C (30°F)** while nights cool to **-8°C (18°F)**. Expect around 3 hours of sunshine per day.

## Rainfall

Toronto sees about **55mm of rainfall across 15 days** during this period. Rainfall is moderate. The odd shower is possible but unlikely to ruin your plans.

## Humidity and Comfort

Average humidity sits at **75%**. Most visitors find this comfortable, though midday can feel slightly muggy.

## What to Pack

- Insulated winter coat with thermal base layers
- Warm hat, gloves, and a scarf
- Insulated waterproof boots
- Compact umbrella or light rain jacket
- Comfortable walking shoes suited to the terrain

## Best Things to Do

- Skate on the rink at Nathan Phillips Square under city lights
- See the city from the CN Tower's glass floor and revolving restaurant
- Explore the St. Lawrence Market for peameal bacon sandwiches and local produce
- Catch a Maple Leafs or Raptors game at Scotiabank Arena

## Travel Tips

- The underground PATH connects 30km of shops and offices — use it to escape the cold
- Wind chill can make it feel 10-15 degrees colder; cover all exposed skin
- TTC day passes are good value if you plan to hop between indoor attractions
